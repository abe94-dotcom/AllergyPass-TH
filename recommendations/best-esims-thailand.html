Why connectivity matters more in Thailand than most places

Thailand's transport infrastructure relies heavily on apps — Grab for rides, Google Maps for navigation, LINE for communication with hotels and locals. Without data, getting around Bangkok or navigating between islands is considerably harder. This isn't a luxury concern; it's a practical one.

The other factor: mobile signal across Thailand's islands is patchier than Bangkok, and some providers that work well in cities drop off on Koh Samui, Koh Phangan, or in northern mountain areas. Coverage differences between networks matter more here than in most countries.

Option 2: Local Thai SIM (recommended for longer stays)

AIS / True Move H (Local SIM)
Best for: stays over 2 weeks, frequent visitors, island travel

AIS and True Move H (formerly DTAC, now merged) are Thailand's two dominant networks. Both are available at dedicated airport counters immediately after customs — look for the branded desks in arrivals. Tourist SIM packages start around ฿299 for 30 days with generous data allowances.

AIS edges out True Move H for rural and island coverage in most independent assessments. For Bangkok and city travel, both are equivalent.

Works well for:
  • Long stays and expat use
  • Island travel (especially Koh Samui, Phuket)
  • Better data value per baht
Limitations:
  • Requires physical SIM — need a slot open on your phone
  • Must be purchased in Thailand

Frequently asked questions

Can I use my home eSIM plan in Thailand?

Most home carrier international plans work in Thailand but are expensive for extended use. Roaming on a US or European plan in Thailand typically costs $10–15/day for data. For any trip over 3 days, a dedicated Thailand eSIM or local SIM is significantly cheaper.

Do I need to register a SIM card in Thailand?

Yes. Since 2022, all SIM cards in Thailand require passport registration. Airport SIM counters handle this at purchase. For eSIMs, registration is handled digitally during setup. Unregistered SIMs purchased from street vendors are technically non-compliant and may be deactivated.

Does Grab work everywhere in Thailand?

Grab operates in Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, and most major tourist cities. In smaller towns and islands, it's less reliable. On Koh Samui, local taxi apps and negotiated fares are more common. You need data for Grab to function — hence the importance of getting connectivity sorted on arrival.

Why this exists

Thai food is full of hidden ingredients.
Menus don't tell you.

Fish sauce is in almost everything. Shrimp paste hides inside curry bases. Peanut oil shows up in stir-fries with no warning. And even if you speak some Thai, explaining a serious food allergy in the middle of a busy restaurant is hard — and the consequences of getting it wrong are serious.

Translation apps aren't reliable for this. A mistranslated allergy is dangerous. A printed card in correct Thai, shown directly to the chef, works.

What to watch for

Common hidden allergens in Thai food

🦐

น้ำปลา — Fish Sauce

In nearly every Thai dish. Rarely listed on menus. A core flavouring, not a garnish.

🌶️

กะปิ — Shrimp Paste

Base ingredient in most curry pastes. Present even when "seafood" isn't on the menu.

🥜

น้ำมันถั่วลิสง — Peanut Oil

Common cooking oil. Street food stalls reuse it across multiple dishes.

🦪

ซอสหอยนางรม — Oyster Sauce

Standard in stir-fries and noodle dishes. Contains both shellfish and gluten.

🌾

ซีอิ๊ว — Soy Sauce

Contains wheat. Used in almost all noodle dishes and many rice dishes.

⚠️

Cross-contamination

Shared woks and oil at street stalls. Common even when the dish itself is "safe."

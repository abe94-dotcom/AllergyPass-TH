Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how AllergyPass collects, uses, and protects information when you use this website, its tools, or its resources. It applies to all pages under allergypass.app.

The short version: AllergyPass collects minimal data, uses it only for what's described here, and doesn't sell or share it with advertisers. The allergy card builder stores all card data locally on your device — nothing is sent to or stored on a server.

If you have questions after reading this, the contact details at the bottom of this page are the right place to start.

Information collected

What gets collected depends on how you use the site. Here's a plain-language breakdown:

What When Why
Email address You sign up for updates or a newsletter To send the content you asked for
Browser and device type You visit any page Anonymous analytics to improve the site
Pages visited, time on site You browse the site Understanding which content is useful
Message content You contact via email or a form To respond to your inquiry
Payment details You make a purchase Handled entirely by third-party processors — not stored here

Card builder data stays on your device. AllergyPass TH stores allergy card information in your browser's local storage only. It is never transmitted to or stored on any server. If you clear your browser data, it is gone — intentionally.

How information is used

Information collected through this site is used for the following purposes only:

  • Delivering digital products or resources you've purchased or requested
  • Sending newsletters or updates you've opted into
  • Responding to questions or support requests
  • Understanding which guides and tools are most useful (via anonymous analytics)
  • Improving the overall site experience
  • Detecting and preventing spam or abuse

Your information is not used for advertising profiling, sold to third parties, or shared with anyone who doesn't need it to deliver a service you've specifically requested.

Cookies and analytics

AllergyPass may use cookies and similar technologies to understand how people use the site. This includes analytics tools that track things like page visits, session duration, and which guides or tools are accessed most.

Analytics data is aggregated and anonymous — it tells us that a page was visited, not who visited it. Services that may be used for this include Google Analytics or similar platforms. These services have their own privacy practices.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings at any time. Doing so may affect some site functionality but will not prevent you from using the allergy card builder, which operates offline without any tracking.

Email communications

If you sign up for updates, a newsletter, or any email resource from AllergyPass, you will only receive what you signed up for.

Every marketing email includes an unsubscribe link. You can opt out at any time, immediately, without needing to contact anyone. Transactional emails (such as purchase receipts) are not marketing and are sent only when necessary.

Email addresses are never shared with other mailing lists or used for anything outside the purpose you provided them for.

Third-party services

AllergyPass uses a small number of third-party services to operate. Depending on how you use the site, these may include:

  • Payment processors (such as Stripe or Lemon Squeezy) — handle all purchase transactions. Payment card information is processed directly by them and is never seen or stored by AllergyPass.
  • Email platforms (such as ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or Mailchimp) — manage newsletter delivery and email signups.
  • Analytics services (such as Google Analytics) — provide anonymous site usage data.
  • Content delivery and hosting — may log basic request data as part of standard server operation.

Each of these services operates under its own privacy policy. AllergyPass selects providers that meet a reasonable standard for data handling, but is not responsible for their independent practices. Links to their policies are available on their respective websites.

Data security

Reasonable technical and operational measures are in place to protect the information that passes through this site. This includes using HTTPS, working with providers that maintain their own security standards, and keeping data collection minimal by design.

No system connected to the internet can guarantee perfect security. What can be controlled is kept as tight as possible. If you have reason to believe your information has been compromised in connection with this site, please get in touch immediately.

Your rights

Depending on where you are located, you may have rights regarding your personal data. In plain terms, you can:

  • Unsubscribe from any email list at any time using the link in any email
  • Request deletion of any personal data held about you
  • Ask what data is held and receive a straightforward answer
  • Correct any inaccurate information

To exercise any of these, email hello@allergypass.app. Requests are handled personally and promptly.

Children's privacy

AllergyPass is not directed at children under the age of 13, and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has submitted personal information through this site, please contact us and it will be removed promptly.

Policy changes

This policy may be updated occasionally — to reflect changes in how the site operates, new services added, or evolving legal requirements. When changes are made, the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page is revised.

Continued use of the site after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. If the changes are significant, a notice will be placed prominently on the site.

Contact

Questions, data requests, or corrections — all handled through the same channel. There's a real person on the other end.

Email: hello@allergypass.app

AllergyPass is not a medical service, healthcare provider, or clinical tool. Nothing on this site constitutes medical advice, and no content here should be treated as such.

Use the tools and information here as a practical supplement, not a replacement for professional medical care.

No guarantee of safety

Food allergy safety in real-world environments is inherently unpredictable. AllergyPass cannot guarantee that following any guide, using any allergy card, or showing any translated phrase will keep you safe in every situation.

You should be aware that:

  • Restaurant menus, ingredients, and kitchen practices can change without notice
  • Cross-contamination risks exist in most food preparation environments
  • Ingredients in dishes may vary between visits, seasons, or locations
  • Translations may not fully capture local dialects, regional terminology, or cultural nuance
  • Staff understanding and kitchen capabilities vary widely between establishments

No allergy card, guide, or communication tool — including those provided by AllergyPass — can guarantee an allergen-free meal or dining experience.

Your responsibility as a user

You are ultimately responsible for the decisions you make about your health, diet, and travel. AllergyPass is a tool to help you prepare and communicate more effectively — but it does not replace your own judgment.

This means you should always:

  • Verify ingredients and preparation methods directly with restaurant staff
  • Communicate your allergies clearly in person, regardless of any card or tool you use
  • Assess cross-contamination and environmental risks yourself before eating
  • Carry appropriate emergency medication (such as an epinephrine auto-injector) if prescribed
  • Make informed personal decisions about the level of risk you're comfortable with

If you are unsure, don't eat it. No travel experience is worth a serious allergic reaction.

Translation limitations

Allergy cards and translated phrases provided by AllergyPass are offered as communication aids only. They are designed to help you convey your needs more clearly in another language — but they are not professionally certified translations, and they may not be perfectly accurate in every situation, dialect, or regional context.

Language is nuanced. Words that convey allergy severity in one context may carry different implications in another. AllergyPass makes reasonable efforts to ensure accuracy, but cannot guarantee that any translated content will be interpreted correctly in every circumstance.

When in doubt, seek additional confirmation through a local interpreter, your hotel, or a medically-trained professional familiar with the local language.

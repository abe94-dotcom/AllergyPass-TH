About

I'm Abe.
A dentist in Bangkok.

I moved to Bangkok and started writing down everything I figured out the hard way. Food, health, safety, daily life. This site is that notebook — made public.

Why a dentist?

Because I actually understand what goes into food, how the body reacts to it, and why hidden ingredients matter more than most people realise. When a friend had a serious allergic reaction at a street food stall in Silom, I built the allergy card tool in a weekend. It helped. So I kept going.

Most Bangkok content is written by tourists. Two-week trips, rented apartments, curated experiences. I live here. I eat here every day. I deal with the same bureaucratic nonsense, the same language barriers, the same "what is actually in this dish" questions that every expat faces eventually.

This site is what I wish had existed when I arrived.

The allergy card

It's a free tool I built and maintain. You enter your allergens, it generates a bilingual Thai and English card you can show to any restaurant or vendor. No sign-up, no cost, works offline. It started as a personal project and people started sharing it — so it lives here now as part of the site.

About

I'm Abe.
A dentist living in Bangkok.

I live here full-time. I write about food, health, and expat life — not as a visitor passing through, but as someone who deals with the consequences of getting it wrong.

Why this exists

Bangkok is easy to enjoy, but not always easy to understand.

Especially when it comes to food. Ingredients get hidden in sauces, pastes, and cooking oils that never show up on a menu. Most people don't think about that until something goes wrong. I think about it by default — it's what dentists do. You learn to read what's going into a body before it becomes a problem.

How it started

A friend had a serious allergic reaction at a street food stall in Silom. She couldn't explain what she needed to avoid. The vendor couldn't understand what she was asking.

So I built a tool to fix that. Then I kept writing about everything else I was figuring out.

What this site is

Most Bangkok content comes from short visits. This doesn't.

I eat here every day. I deal with confusing menus, bureaucratic friction, and regular moments of "wait, what is actually in this." This site is a record of that.

What's here

Food and restaurants. What's in Thai food, how ordering actually works, and what to watch for if you're avoiding specific ingredients.

Expat life. Banking, SIM cards, visas, healthcare. The things that take longer than expected.

Things to do. Neighbourhoods, markets, day trips. Some better in practice than they sound, some the opposite.

The allergy card

A free tool I built and maintain. You enter your allergens and it generates a card in Thai and English to show a vendor or waiter. It focuses on the ingredients that don't appear on menus — fish sauce, shrimp paste, peanut oil — because those are the ones that cause problems.

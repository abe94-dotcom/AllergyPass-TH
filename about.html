About

I'm Abe.
A healthcare professional living in Thailand.

I write about Thailand — and build tools for it — because most of what's out there isn't built for people who actually deal with the place.

Why this exists

Thailand is not a difficult country. But there are specific things — food ingredients, healthcare systems, practical logistics — that are harder to navigate than they appear, especially for people arriving with health considerations or longer-term plans.

Most content about Thailand is written by people who visited for two weeks. The advice reflects that. It covers the easy surface, not the situations that actually catch people out.

How it started

A friend had a serious allergic reaction at a street stall in Bangkok. She couldn't explain what she needed to avoid. The vendor couldn't understand what she was asking. That problem has a fix — a written card in Thai that names specific allergens clearly. So I built one.

That became AllergyPass TH. Then I kept writing about everything else I was figuring out.

What this site is

A practical Thailand platform. Tools that solve specific problems. Guides based on real experience, not research done from somewhere else. Recommendations that I've actually used or investigated in enough depth to stand behind.

The scope is Thailand-wide — Bangkok is where I'm based, but the tools and guides are designed to work across the country.

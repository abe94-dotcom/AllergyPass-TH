About

I'm Abe.
A dentist in Bangkok.

I live here full-time. This site is a practical guide to how Bangkok actually works — food, health, expat systems, daily life. Not tourism content.

Why a dentist?

I understand what goes into food, how the body reacts to it, and why hidden ingredients matter more than most people realise. That background changes how I read a menu.

When a friend had a serious allergic reaction at a street food stall in Silom, the problem was simple: she didn't know what to ask, and the vendor didn't understand what was being asked. I built the allergy card to fix that specific gap. The rest of the site grew from the same logic — Bangkok has a lot of gaps like that.

Most Bangkok content is written by people passing through. Two-week trips, curated experiences, content shot in a day. I eat here every day. I deal with the same language barriers, the same bureaucratic friction, the same "what is actually in this dish" questions that anyone living here eventually runs into.

The allergy card

A free tool. You enter your allergens, it generates a bilingual Thai and English card to show any restaurant or vendor. No account, no cost, works offline. It accounts for hidden ingredients specific to Thai cooking — fish sauce, shrimp paste, peanut oil — the things that don't appear on any menu but show up in most dishes.

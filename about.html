About

I'm Abe. A healthcare professional
living in Bangkok.

I build practical tools and write about travel, food safety, healthcare, and navigating unfamiliar places properly.

Why this exists

Most travel content is built around short visits and surface-level experiences. That works fine until something actually matters.

Food allergies. Medical situations. Language barriers. Understanding what you're eating. Navigating healthcare systems in countries you don't fully understand yet.

Those are the situations people usually figure out the hard way. This site exists to make them easier to navigate before you're in them.

How AllergyPass started

A friend of mine had a serious allergic reaction at a street food stall in Bangkok.

She tried explaining what she needed to avoid, but the communication wasn't clear enough. The vendor was trying to help. She was trying to be careful. It still went wrong.

The solution was surprisingly simple: a written allergy card that clearly explained specific ingredients and allergens in the local language.

So I built one. That became AllergyPass.

What started as a practical allergy communication tool eventually grew into something broader. A place for tools, guides, and useful information focused on the real side of travel and living abroad.

What this site is

Part travel resource, part practical toolkit. Bangkok is the origin and the base, but the intent is broader than one country.

I write about food safety, healthcare, travel logistics, local systems, and the details people usually only learn after spending real time somewhere. Some of that is specific to Thailand because that's where I live and what I know best. But the bigger idea scales to anywhere unfamiliar.

The internet already has enough generic travel advice. I'm more interested in useful information people can actually rely on when something goes wrong.

What's here

AllergyPass. A multilingual allergy communication tool designed to help travelers communicate food restrictions clearly and safely, in the language of wherever they happen to be eating.

Guides and blog posts. Food ingredients, allergy safety, healthcare navigation, travel logistics, and practical advice based on real experience. Not written to rank, written to be useful.

Recommendations. Apps, eSIMs, services, and products I've personally used or researched thoroughly enough to stand behind. Affiliate-supported, but only things worth including.

More to come. Additional tools and resources are in development.

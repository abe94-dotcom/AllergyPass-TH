About

I'm Abe.
A dentist in Bangkok.

I live here full-time. I write about food, health, and expat life from the perspective of someone who can't leave when it gets inconvenient.

Why a dentist?

I know what goes into food and what the body does with it. That's more relevant in Bangkok than it sounds. Ingredients get hidden in sauces, pastes, and cooking oils that don't show up on any menu. Most people don't think about this until something goes wrong.

A friend had a serious allergic reaction at a street food stall in Silom. She didn't know how to ask about the ingredients. The vendor didn't understand what she was asking. I built a tool to fix that specific problem, and kept writing about the other things I was figuring out along the way.

Most Bangkok content is written by people who were here for two weeks. I eat here every day. I deal with the same confusing menus, the same bureaucratic friction, the same moments of "wait, what is actually in this." That's where most of the writing on this site comes from.

What's here

Food and restaurants. What's in Thai dishes, how to order if you have dietary restrictions, and reviews of places I go to regularly.

Expat life. Banking, SIM cards, visas, healthcare. The things that take too long to figure out on your own.

Things to do. Neighbourhoods, markets, day trips. I've been wrong about a few of them and updated accordingly.

The allergy card

A free tool. You enter your allergens and it generates a card in Thai and English to hand to a vendor or waiter. It covers the ingredients that don't appear on menus — fish sauce, shrimp paste, peanut oil — because those are the ones that cause problems.

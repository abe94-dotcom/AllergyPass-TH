Why this exists

Thai food is full of hidden ingredients.
Menus don't tell you.

Fish sauce is in almost everything. Shrimp paste hides inside curry bases. Peanut oil shows up in stir-fries with no warning. And even if you speak some Thai, explaining a serious food allergy in the middle of a busy restaurant is hard — and the consequences of getting it wrong are serious.

Translation apps aren't built for this. A mistranslated allergy is dangerous. A written card in correct Thai, shown directly to the chef, works.

What to watch for

Common hidden allergens
in Thai food

🐟

น้ำปลา — Fish Sauce

In nearly every Thai dish. Rarely listed on menus. Functions as salt in Thai cooking.

🦐

กะปิ — Shrimp Paste

Base ingredient in most curry pastes. Present even when "seafood" isn't on the menu.

🥜

ถั่วลิสง — Peanuts

Common cooking fat and garnish. Street food stalls reuse shared woks across dishes.

🦪

ซอสหอยนางรม — Oyster Sauce

Standard in stir-fries and noodle dishes. Contains both shellfish and gluten.

🌾

ซีอิ๊ว — Soy Sauce

Contains wheat and soy. Used in almost all noodle dishes and many stir-fries.

⚠️

Cross-contamination

Shared woks and cooking oil at street stalls. Common even in dishes that seem safe.

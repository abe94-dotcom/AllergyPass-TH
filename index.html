The problem

Eating out in Thailand with an allergy is risky.

🗣️

Language barriers

Most Thai street vendors and small restaurants speak little English. Explaining allergies verbally is unreliable and stressful.

🫙

Hidden ingredients

Shrimp paste, fish sauce, peanut oil, and oyster sauce are invisible in most Thai dishes — even "safe" ones.

⚠️

Shared cooking

Street food kitchens use the same wok, oil, and utensils for everything. Cross-contamination is extremely common.

📱

Translation apps fail

Google Translate doesn't warn about hidden ingredients. A translated phrase rarely conveys the severity of an anaphylactic allergy.

The solution

A card designed to be read at a glance.

AllergyCard TH generates a pre-translated allergy card in Thai and English. Hand it to the chef — no conversation needed.

  • Thai text, large and bold — readable in 2 seconds
  • Lists hidden ingredients specific to your allergen
  • Shows severity (anaphylactic, severe, intolerance)
  • Works offline — no internet at the restaurant needed
  • Saves to your phone home screen as an app

Common questions

Things people actually need to know

What hidden allergens are common in Thai food?

Fish sauce (น้ำปลา) and shrimp paste (กะปิ) appear in the vast majority of Thai curries, stir-fries, and dipping sauces — including dishes that don't mention seafood on the menu. Peanuts and peanut oil are used broadly as cooking fat and garnish. Dried shrimp appears in som tam, fried rice, and many street dishes. These four account for most allergic incidents in Thai restaurants.

How do I tell Thai restaurant staff about a food allergy?

A written card in Thai is significantly more reliable than spoken requests. Verbal English explanations are frequently misunderstood, especially at street stalls. A card that names allergens in Thai — including hidden ingredients like fish sauce and shrimp paste — changes that conversation entirely.

What should I do in a medical emergency in Thailand?

Dial 1669 for national EMS. Private hospitals including Bumrungrad, Bangkok Hospital, and Samitivej provide internationally accredited emergency care in major cities. Travel insurance with emergency evacuation coverage is strongly recommended before arriving.

What's the best eSIM or SIM card for Thailand?

AIS and True Move H offer the best coverage nationally, including islands and rural areas. For short trips, Airalo or Holafly eSIMs work well without a Thai SIM. For stays beyond two weeks, a local Thai SIM from the airport offers significantly better data pricing.
